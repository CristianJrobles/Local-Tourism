History

Aguachica, a vibrant municipality in the department of Cesar, Colombia, is a place where history, culture, and natural beauty intertwine. Founded on August 16, 1748, by Captain José Lázaro de Rivera, Aguachica began as a small settlement on the banks of the Magdalena River, a crucial waterway that shaped its early development.
Throughout its history, Aguachica has been an agricultural center, with its fertile lands supporting crops such as cotton, rice, and livestock. Its strategic location along important trade routes, such as the historic Camino Real, made it a key commercial center in the region, connecting the Caribbean coast with the Colombian interior.
In the 20th century, Aguachica experienced rapid growth, becoming the second largest city in Cesar after Valledupar. Its economy expanded with the rise of the palm oil industry, and the town became known for its warm, welcoming people and lively festivals, such as the Fiesta de San Roque, which attracts visitors from across the region. Landmarks like the iconic turtle statue—a symbol of the local wildlife—and the historic San Roque Church highlight Aguachica's pride in its natural and spiritual heritage.

Waffles

Broster Chicken

Fried chicken, or broaster chicken, is a dish consisting of pieces of tender chicken meat, sometimes coated in flour or batter, and then deep-fried. The breading adds a crispy outer crust that prevents the loss of juices but also absorbs the fat used during frying.

Patron saint festivities of Aguachica, Cesar

The patron saint festivals of Aguachica, Cesar, are celebrations in honor of San Roque, the city's patron saint. These festivals, held annually, typically include parades, music, dancing, and cultural activities that highlight the local identity. The Aguachica City Hall, through the Municipal Institute of Sports, Recreation, and Culture (IMDREC), usually organizes the cultural programming for these festivals.

  • Aguachica's patron saint festivities are usually held in August, commemorating the city's founding date and the feast of San Roque.
  • Parades, folklore group performances, dances, and cultural activities showcase the region's richness.
